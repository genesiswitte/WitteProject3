Stefan Sagmeister and Jessica Walsh’s work is featured in museums across the world. Together and individually, these two have won numerous prestigious awards and continue on with their legacy to this day. However, in 2019 after the Beauty exhibition, Jessica decided to stray from Sagmeister & Walsh to start her own creative agency that she named, &Walsh. While they may not be designing together anymore, they will forever recognized and known in history for their iconic design duo and groundbreaking design work.

Jessica lectures about design at creative conferences and universities internationally. She teaches design and typography at The School of Visual Arts in NYC. She has won some major design competitions including Type Director’s Club, Art Director’s Club, SPD, Print, New York Festivals, D&AD, TDC Tokyo, and Graphis, amongst many others. Her work has been featured in numerous books & magazines.. Clients include: Museum of Modern Art, The Jewish Museum, Institute of Contemporary Art Philadelphia, Barneys, The New York Times, Levis, Adobe, and The School of Visual Arts. She continues to work for clients as well as personal work now for her own design studio, &Walsh.

Stefan Sagmeister has won practically every international design award. He has worked for a wide range of well-known clients including the Rolling Stones, HBO, and the Guggenheim museum. Having been nominated eight times, he finally won 2 Grammies for the Talking Heads and Brian Eno & David Byrne package designs. Solo shows on his work have been mounted all over the world, and his exhibition “The Happy Show” attracted well over half a million visitors worldwide, becoming the most visited graphic design show in history.
